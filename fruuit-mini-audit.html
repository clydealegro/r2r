Section 02
The 3 Things Costing You Clients Right Now
Problem 01

Your Hero Headline Could Be Any Consulting Firm's — There's Zero Differentiation

What's happening

The first thing visitors see is "World Class Sustainability Expertise." That's the kind of line McKinsey, Deloitte, or a one-person shop could all use. It says nothing about what Fruuit actually does or why it's different. The real story — that your team literally built ESG rating methodologies at MSCI and Sustainalytics — is right there in the hero, but it sits below the headline in smaller text. Most visitors won't read past a generic headline to find it. The one thing that makes Fruuit genuinely rare in this space isn't leading.

Why it costs you clients

A VP of Sustainability lands on this page from a Google search or LinkedIn link. They've probably looked at 3-4 ESG consulting sites already this week. If your headline doesn't immediately signal something different, they bounce in under 8 seconds. They never reach the subheadline where the actual differentiator lives. You're losing the visitors most likely to buy because the hook doesn't hook.

The fix (preview)

The insider story is your credibility hook, but a headline also needs to tell visitors what you do for them in one read. The full audit covers how to combine both into a single line that differentiates and clarifies.

Problem 02

Your Homepage Has Zero Social Proof — Not a Single Testimonial, Logo, or Result

What's happening

The stat bar claims "dozens of ESG ratings improvement success stories" and "10+ countries" — but the homepage shows exactly zero evidence. No testimonials. No client logos. No before/after rating improvements. No case study snippets. Proof exists on a separate Testimonials page, but that requires a visitor to actively seek it out through the nav. Most won't.

Why it costs you clients

Decision-makers evaluating a $25K–$100K+ engagement need evidence before they fill out a contact form. Unsubstantiated claims in a stat bar actually hurt credibility more than they help — they read as marketing copy rather than proof. The contact form asks for budget commitment. Without a single named testimonial or result visible on the page, visitors don't have enough trust to take that step.

The fix (preview)

Even one specific, named testimonial placed above the contact form would change the conversion math for this page. The full audit covers proof hierarchy — what types of evidence work best for ESG consulting buyers and exactly where to position them in the page flow.

Problem 03

Your Contact Form Asks for Budget Before You've Built Any Trust

What's happening

The main conversion path is a contact form asking for budget range ($25K–$100K+), desired start date, and a detailed message. That's a high-commitment ask. Meanwhile, the lower-friction "Book a Call" button is tucked at the bottom of the page, after the contact form, and in the nav bar — not positioned as the primary action. The page structure puts the hardest ask first and the easiest ask last.

Why it costs you clients

Professional services conversion follows a trust ladder. A visitor who just arrived doesn't know if Fruuit is the right fit yet. Asking them to declare budget before they've had a conversation feels presumptuous. It filters out exactly the high-value prospects who are evaluating options carefully. A 15-minute call is almost always an easier yes than a form that asks someone to declare budget before they've even spoken to you.

The fix (preview)

The CTA hierarchy needs restructuring. The full audit includes specific recommendations for conversion path design — which asks go where, and how to lower friction without lowering quality.

Section 03
What You're Doing Well
✓

The MSCI/Sustainalytics origin story in the subheadline is a genuinely powerful differentiator — you built the systems your clients are trying to master. That's rare positioning that most competitors can't claim.

✓

Clean, professional visual design that signals credibility. The Salient theme is well-configured, green brand palette feels on-brand for ESG, and the SVG animations add polish without feeling gimmicky.

✓

The NDA checkbox on the contact form shows you understand your buyers. Enterprise ESG teams deal with sensitive pre-disclosure data — offering an NDA upfront signals that you get their world.

✓

Four clearly defined service offerings (Ratings Consultant, Fractional Analyst, Advisory, Investor Integration) cover distinct use cases without overlap. Visitors can self-identify which one fits their situation.
