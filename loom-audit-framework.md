# LOOM VIDEO AUDIT FRAMEWORK
# Version 1.0 — Paired with Mini-Audit Outreach Document
# Target length: 3-5 minutes (HARD CEILING: 5 minutes)

---

## HOW THE LOOM + MINI-AUDIT WORK TOGETHER

The Loom and the mini-audit are two halves of one system. They are NOT redundant.
They each do a different job:

| | LOOM VIDEO | MINI-AUDIT DOC |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary job** | Build trust and likability | Build credibility and authority |
| **Shows** | Your face, your thinking process | Your system, your depth |
| **Tone** | Conversational, warm, peer-to-peer | Precise, data-driven, strategic |
| **Format** | Walk-through with live reactions | Structured scorecard with findings |
| **Strength** | Makes them feel seen as a person | Makes them feel exposed as a business |
| **Creates** | "I like this person" | "This person knows what they're doing" |
| **Bridges to** | "I also put together a scorecard..." | "The video walks through this in more detail..." |

**The bridge lines are critical.** In the Loom, you reference the mini-audit doc.
In the mini-audit doc, you don't reference the Loom (it's a standalone deliverable).
The Loom is the warm handshake. The doc is the business card they keep.

---

## PRE-RECORDING CHECKLIST

Before you hit record:

- [ ] Run the mini-audit prompt FIRST — have the scorecard and 3 problems identified
- [ ] Have their website open in a clean browser tab (no bookmarks bar, no other tabs visible)
- [ ] Your face is visible in the Loom bubble (bottom-left or bottom-right)
- [ ] Clean background, decent lighting, no distractions
- [ ] You know their company name, what they do, and who their clients are (30 seconds of research)
- [ ] You know your 3 key points (from the mini-audit) — you're not discovering them live
- [ ] No notes visible on screen — this should LOOK spontaneous even though you've prepared

**Important:** You already know the findings from running the mini-audit. The Loom is a
PERFORMANCE of discovery, not actual discovery. You're walking them through what you found
as if you're seeing it fresh, but you're not fumbling or searching — you're precise.

## THE FRAMEWORK (5 BEATS)

Think of the Loom as a 5-beat story. Each beat has a job, a target length,
and talking points. This is a framework, NOT a script — use your natural voice.

---

### BEAT 1 — THE OPEN (15-20 seconds)

**Job:** Establish who you are, why you're reaching out, and that this is personal.

**What to say (your words, not a script):**

- Your name
- What you do (one sentence — "I focus on conversion optimization for ESG consulting websites")
- Why you're looking at THEIR site specifically (connection point)
- What's coming ("I put together a quick scorecard and wanted to walk you through what I found")

**Framework:**

> "Hey [First Name], Clyde here. I work with ESG consulting firms on their
> websites — specifically making them better at converting visitors into
> booked calls. I came across [Company Name] and took a look at your site
> this week, and I wanted to share a few things I noticed."

**Rules:**
- Say their name and their company name in the first 10 seconds
- Do NOT say "I'm the founder of OXVN" — nobody cares about your agency name in a cold outreach
- Do NOT pitch. You're sharing, not selling.
- Keep it under 20 seconds. People decide whether to keep watching in the first 15.

---

### BEAT 2 — THE FIRST SCROLL (30-45 seconds)

**Job:** Walk through the homepage from the top. React to what you see.
Let the prospect see their own site through your expert eyes.

**What to do:**

- Share your screen showing their homepage
- Start at the top — the hero section
- Scroll slowly down the page as you talk
- Point out 1-2 things that are WORKING first (this is important — lead with something positive)

**Framework:**

> "So here's your homepage. First thing I notice — [something positive].
> That's good because [why it matters]. Your [specific element] also
> does a nice job of [function]."

**Why lead positive:**
- It proves you actually looked (you're not just bashing them)
- It lowers their defenses before you get to the problems
- It shows balanced judgment — you're a peer, not a critic

**Rules:**
- Actually move your cursor and point at things — visual engagement matters
- Be specific. "Your hero image is clean and professional" is better than "nice design"
- Limit to 1-2 positives — don't oversell what's working or you'll undermine the problems

---

### BEAT 3 — THE THREE PROBLEMS (2-2.5 minutes)

**Job:** Walk through the 3 biggest conversion problems. This is the core of the video.
These are the SAME 3 problems from the mini-audit doc, but explained conversationally.

**Structure: Spend 40-50 seconds on each problem.**

For each problem:

1. **SHOW IT** (5-10 sec) — Scroll to or highlight the exact part of the page.
   Point with your cursor. "Right here, this section..."

2. **NAME IT** (5-10 sec) — State the problem clearly in one sentence.
   "The issue is [specific problem]."

3. **EXPLAIN WHY IT MATTERS** (15-20 sec) — Connect it to their buyer's experience.
   "When a VP of sustainability lands on this page, the first thing they're looking
   for is [X]. Right now, they're seeing [Y] instead. That gap is where you lose them."

4. **HINT AT THE FIX** (10-15 sec) — Show you know how to solve it without solving it.
   "There's a straightforward way to restructure this that I've seen work really well
   for firms in your space..." or "The fix here is actually about the messaging hierarchy,
   not the design — I get into it more in the scorecard."

**THE BRIDGE TO THE MINI-AUDIT:** On at least one of the three problems, say something like:
> "I broke this down in more detail in the scorecard I put together — you'll see the
> specific scoring and what's behind the numbers."

This gives them a reason to open the attachment/link.

**Rules for Beat 3:**
- Be SPECIFIC. Point at actual headlines, actual buttons, actual sections on THEIR site.
- Use their prospect's language. "Your clients — the sustainability directors, the CFOs
  facing CSRD deadlines — they land here and they see..."
- Don't be mean or condescending. Tone is: "Here's what I'd change if this were my site."
- Don't give full solutions. The fix is in the full audit. The Loom shows you see the problem.
- Move your cursor constantly. Dead screen = dead attention.

**Problem transition phrases (keep it flowing):**
- "The second thing that jumped out to me..."
- "This one's probably the biggest opportunity..."
- "And the third thing, which ties into the first..."

---

### BEAT 4 — THE SCORE DROP (20-30 seconds)

**Job:** Reveal the overall score. This is the emotional peak of the video.
A number is concrete, memorable, and shareable ("Our site scored 38 out of 100").

**Framework:**

> "So when I put all of this together — the messaging, the structure, the CTAs,
> all 10 areas I look at — your site scores [X] out of 100. For context, most
> ESG consulting sites I've reviewed land somewhere between 30 and 50. So you're
> [right in that range / slightly below / slightly above average for the space]."

**Then immediately reframe as OPPORTUNITY, not criticism:**

> "The good news is that the firms that fix even 2-3 of these things usually see
> a significant jump in the number of qualified conversations coming through their site.
> The expertise is clearly there — it's the translation layer between your expertise
> and your buyer's decision that needs work."

**Rules:**
- State the number clearly. Don't hedge it.
- Always provide context ("most sites in your space score X"). A raw number without
  context is meaningless.
- Immediately pivot to opportunity. You're not here to make them feel bad.
  You're here to show them what's possible.
- Use the phrase "translation layer" — it positions the problem as a communication
  issue, not a competence issue. Nobody gets defensive about their website's
  communication skills.

---

### BEAT 5 — THE CLOSE (20-30 seconds)

**Job:** Tell them what to do next. Reference the mini-audit doc. Soft CTA.

**Framework:**

> "I put together a scorecard that breaks down all 10 areas with the scores
> and the three biggest things I'd prioritize. I'll [attach it / drop the link]
> in the message.
>
> If any of this resonates and you want to dig deeper, I'm happy to chat —
> no pitch, just walk through what the full picture looks like. Either way,
> hope this was useful. Talk soon."

**Rules:**
- Reference the mini-audit doc explicitly — "I put together a scorecard" / "attached the full breakdown"
- The CTA is SOFT. "Happy to chat" not "Book a call with me."
- End with warmth. "Hope this was useful" is genuine and non-pushy.
- Do NOT say "Let me know if you have any questions" — that's weak and puts
  the burden on them. "Happy to chat if any of this resonates" is active and specific.
- Do NOT pitch your services, your agency, your pricing, or your process.
  The video is a gift. The sale happens later.

## TIMING GUIDE

| Beat | Target | Max |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1. The Open | 15 sec | 20 sec |
| 2. First Scroll (positives) | 30 sec | 45 sec |
| 3. Three Problems | 2 min | 2.5 min |
| 4. Score Drop | 20 sec | 30 sec |
| 5. The Close | 20 sec | 30 sec |
| **TOTAL** | **~3.5 min** | **5 min** |

If you're over 5 minutes, you're rambling. Cut a problem detail, not a beat.

## DELIVERY NOTES

**Energy:**
- Calm, confident, conversational. Like you're showing a colleague something
  interesting you found — not presenting to a boardroom.
- No vocal fry. No uptalk. No "um" fillers (pause silently instead).
- Speak slightly slower than your natural pace. Loom audio compresses fast speech.

**Face bubble:**
- Keep it visible the entire time. Your face builds trust.
- Look at the camera (your webcam) when you're making a key point.
  Look at the screen when you're pointing at something.
- Subtle expressions matter — a slight brow raise when you spot a problem,
  a nod when you see something working. These are trust micro-signals.

**Screen:**
- Browser in full screen, no tabs, no bookmarks bar
- Zoom the page to ~110-125% so text is readable in the recording
- Move your cursor deliberately — use it as a pointer, not a fidget toy
- When highlighting a specific element, slow your cursor down and circle it once

**Recording tips:**
- Record in one take. Don't over-produce. A slightly imperfect Loom feels
  more authentic than a polished one. If you stumble on a word, keep going.
- Do NOT re-record more than twice. The third take always sounds rehearsed.
- Watch your own video before sending. Check: is my face visible, is the
  audio clear, did I say their company name, is it under 5 minutes?

## THE COMPLETE OUTREACH SEQUENCE

Here's how all three pieces work together:

```
1. RUN mini-audit prompt → generates scorecard + 3 problems + score
2. RECORD Loom using this framework → references the same 3 problems + score
3. SEND outreach message:
   - LinkedIn DM or email
   - 4-5 lines max
   - Links to Loom
   - Attaches or links to mini-audit HTML/PDF
   
4. WAIT 3-4 business days

5. FOLLOW UP (if no response):
   - "Hey [Name], just checking if you had a chance to see the walkthrough
     I sent over. No worries either way — happy to chat if it sparked
     any questions."
   - ONE follow-up only. Don't chase.

6. IF THEY RESPOND:
   - Offer a 15-minute call to "walk through the full picture"
   - On the call, pitch the full CRO audit as the logical next step
   - Full audit = paid engagement ($X,000) that includes everything
     in your Master Prompt v2.4
```

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- **Don't wing it.** Always run the mini-audit prompt first. The Loom should feel
  spontaneous but be prepared. Know your 3 points before you hit record.

- **Don't go over 5 minutes.** Nobody watches a 10-minute Loom from a stranger.
  3-4 minutes is the sweet spot. Respect their time and they'll respect your expertise.

- **Don't be negative.** You're not roasting their site. You're showing them
  what's possible. Every problem should feel like an opportunity, not an insult.

- **Don't sell.** The Loom sells by not selling. The moment you pitch your services,
  you become a salesperson. Stay in expert/peer mode the entire time.

- **Don't show your audit framework on screen.** They should never see the
  scoring system, the prompt, or your process. They see the RESULT of your
  process (the scorecard), not the process itself. The system is your IP.

- **Don't compare them to competitors by name.** "Sites in your space" is fine.
  "Your competitor [Name] does this better" is unprofessional and makes them
  defensive. You're auditing their site, not ranking the industry.

- **Don't record without doing your homework.** Know what the company does,
  who their clients are, and what frameworks they work with (CSRD, GRI, TCFD, etc.)
  before you record. Getting a basic fact wrong kills all credibility instantly.

## PRACTICE REPS

Before sending your first real outreach Loom, do 2-3 practice recordings:

1. **Practice Loom 1:** Pick any ESG consulting site from your list. Record the
   full 5-beat framework. Watch it back. Note where you ramble, where you're
   unclear, where you sound scripted.

2. **Practice Loom 2:** Different site. Focus on fixing what you noticed in #1.
   Time yourself — aim for under 4 minutes.

3. **Practice Loom 3:** Different site. This one should feel natural. If it does,
   you're ready to send the first real one.

Delete the practice Looms. They're warm-ups, not deliverables.
