# CRO MINI-AUDIT — OUTREACH VERSION
# Version 1.0 — Paired with Loom Video for Cold/Warm Outreach
# Purpose: Generate a concise, high-impact audit teaser that makes prospects want more.

---

## PURPOSE & STRATEGY

This is NOT the full audit. This is the outreach hook.

The full CRO Audit Master Prompt (v2.4) is your paid deliverable — the thing clients get
when they engage you. This mini-audit is the free taste that makes them hungry for the meal.

**What it does:**
- Shows the prospect you've actually studied their website (not a template email)
- Reveals 3 specific, high-impact problems they didn't know they had
- Demonstrates your expertise without giving away the full playbook
- Creates a natural next step: "Want me to do the full audit?"

**Paired with:** A 3-5 minute Loom video walking through the findings. The Loom is the
personality and warmth. The mini-audit doc is the credibility and takeaway they can
share with their team.

## 📊 OUTPUT: THE MINI-AUDIT

Generate a single-page HTML file with these 4 sections ONLY.
This is a tight, punchy document — not a comprehensive report.

---

### SECTION 1 — SCORECARD SNAPSHOT

Score all 10 areas on a 1-10 scale. Display as a visual dashboard grid.
Use the same 10 areas as the Master Prompt:

1. Hero / Above the Fold
2. Messaging Clarity
3. Social Proof
4. CTA Strategy
5. Page Flow & Structure
6. Trust & Credibility
7. Visual Design / UX
8. Objection Handling
9. Audience Segmentation
10. Urgency / Compliance Hook

For each area show:
- Area name
- Score: X/10
- Visual score bar
- Status: 🟢 Strong (8-10) | 🟡 Needs Work (5-7) | 🔴 Critical (1-4)

Then show:
- OVERALL SCORE: X/100
- Verdict: Strong (75+) | Needs Work (50-74) | Critical (<50)
- 1 sentence overall assessment — sharp, specific, no fluff

**The scorecard is the hook.** Most ESG consulting sites score 30-50. When a founder
sees a big red number, they want to know why and how to fix it.

---

### SECTION 2 — THE 3 THINGS COSTING YOU CLIENTS RIGHT NOW

This is the core of the mini-audit. Identify the 3 highest-impact conversion problems.

For each problem, use this exact structure:

**[PROBLEM TITLE]** (sharp, specific — e.g. "Your Hero Says Nothing About What You Actually Do")

- **What's happening:** 2-3 sentences describing the specific problem on THEIR page.
  Reference actual elements, actual copy, actual layout decisions. Not generic observations.
  
- **Why it costs you clients:** 2-3 sentences connecting this to their buyer's psychology.
  Who is the visitor? What are they thinking? Why does this problem make them leave?
  
- **The fix (preview):** 1-2 sentences hinting at the solution WITHOUT giving the full
  rewrite. Enough to show you know exactly what to do. Not enough that they can do it
  themselves without you.

**Rules for the 3 problems:**
- Each must be SPECIFIC to their website — not applicable to any random site
- At least one must be about messaging/copy (this is where you shine)
- At least one must be about page structure or CTA strategy
- Never say "your website needs improvement" — say exactly WHAT is broken and WHY
- The "fix preview" creates a curiosity gap, not a tutorial

**Examples of GOOD problem titles:**
- "Your Hero Headline Could Be Any Consulting Firm's — There's Zero Differentiation"
- "You Have No Conversion Path Between 'Services' and 'Contact' — Visitors Drop Off"
- "Your Social Proof Is Buried Below 3 Screens of Text Nobody Reads"
- "Your CTA Says 'Contact Us' — The Least Compelling Ask in Professional Services"

**Examples of BAD problem titles (too generic):**
- "Improve Your Headline"
- "Add More Social Proof"
- "Better CTA Needed"

---

### SECTION 3 — WHAT YOU'RE DOING WELL

2-4 specific things that ARE working on the site. This is important because:
1. It proves you actually looked at the site, not just ran a template
2. It shows balanced judgment, not just criticism
3. It builds trust — you're not tearing them down, you're giving an honest assessment

Each item: 1 sentence, specific. Reference actual page elements.

---

to turn your site into your best-performing sales asset.

## 📏 QUALITY STANDARDS

**Specificity rule:** Every observation must reference something specific on THEIR page.
If you could copy-paste the same finding onto 10 different sites, it's too generic.

**Honesty rule:** Scores must be accurate. Don't artificially deflate to create urgency.
Most ESG sites genuinely score 30-50, so you don't need to exaggerate.

**Curiosity gap rule:** The "fix preview" in Section 2 must create desire for the full
audit without giving away the implementation. Show that you KNOW the answer without
fully REVEALING the answer.

**Tone rule:** Confident, direct, collegial. You're a peer who happens to be an expert
in this specific thing, not a salesperson. No hype language. No "game-changing."
No exclamation points. Let the findings speak.

**Length rule:** The entire mini-audit should fit in roughly 1-2 scrolls on a laptop screen.
If it's longer, you're giving away too much. Trim ruthlessly.

## 🚫 ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS

- DO NOT include full copy rewrites (that's the paid audit)
- DO NOT include wireframes or page reconstructions (that's the paid audit)
- DO NOT include the full action plan (that's the paid audit)
- DO NOT include persona analysis (that's the paid audit)
- DO NOT use generic observations that could apply to any website
- DO NOT use an aggressive sales tone in Section 4
- DO NOT inflate or deflate scores to manipulate
- DO NOT output as chat text — HTML file ONLY
- DO NOT make the document longer than ~800 words of content

---

## 🎯 THE GOAL

The mini-audit + Loom combo should make the prospect think exactly three things:

1. "This person actually looked at MY website." (Specificity)
2. "I didn't realize those problems were there." (New insight)
3. "I want to see the full version of this." (Curiosity gap)

If it achieves all three, you'll get a reply. If it only achieves one or two, you won't.
