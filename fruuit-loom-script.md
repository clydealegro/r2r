# LOOM SCRIPT — Fruuit Consulting
### Target: 4–5 minutes | Record in one take

---

## BEAT 1 — THE OPEN (15 seconds)

*[Camera on your face. No screen share yet.]*

"Hey, this is Clyde. I work with ESG consulting firms on their websites — specifically making them convert more visitors into booked calls. I came across Fruuit Consulting this week and took a look at your site, and I wanted to share a few things I noticed."

*[Start screen share — fruuitconsulting.com homepage loaded, clean browser, no tabs.]*

---

## BEAT 2 — THE FIRST SCROLL (30–40 seconds)

*[Screen share is live. Slowly scroll from the top of the homepage.]*

"So here's your homepage. First thing — the visual design is clean. The green palette feels right for ESG, the layout is professional, the SVG animations add polish without being distracting. It looks credible. That matters.

I also like that you have four clearly defined service offerings — Ratings Consultant, Fractional Analyst, Advisory, Investor Integration. A visitor can self-select pretty quickly which one fits their situation. That's not always the case with consulting sites."

*[Scroll back up to hero.]*

---

## BEAT 3 — THE THREE PROBLEMS (2–2.5 minutes)

### Problem 1 (~45 seconds)

*[Cursor pointing at the hero headline.]*

"Here's where it gets interesting. Your headline says 'World Class Sustainability Expertise.' And the issue is — McKinsey could say that. Deloitte could say that. A one-person shop could say that. It doesn't tell me anything specific about Fruuit.

But right below it, in smaller text, you mention that your team built ESG rating methodologies at MSCI and Sustainalytics. That's a rare claim. Most firms in this space can't say that. The problem is it's buried underneath a generic headline. A VP of Sustainability who's looked at three other consulting sites this week reads 'World Class Sustainability Expertise,' and they bounce in under eight seconds. They never reach the line that would've made them stay.

There's a way to restructure this so the insider story leads — I get into it more in the scorecard I put together."

### Problem 2 (~45 seconds)

*[Scroll down the page. Cursor moving across sections, pointing at the stat bar area.]*

"The second thing that jumped out to me — your homepage has zero social proof. No testimonials, no client logos, no before-and-after rating improvements. Nothing.

You've got a stat bar that says 'dozens of ESG ratings improvement success stories' and '10+ countries.' But claims without evidence actually hurt more than they help. They read as marketing copy. I checked your nav — you do have a separate Testimonials page. But here's the thing: most visitors won't click through to find it. They need to see proof on the page they're already on, especially before they're asked to fill out a form.

Even one specific, named testimonial placed above the contact form would change the math on this page. The scorecard breaks down exactly where proof should sit in the page flow."

### Problem 3 (~45 seconds)

*[Scroll down to the contact form. Cursor circling the budget dropdown and form fields.]*

"And the third thing, which ties into the first two. Your main conversion path is this contact form — and it asks for budget range, from $25K up to $100K-plus, a desired start date, and a detailed message. That's a big ask from someone who just arrived on your site.

Meanwhile, the lower-friction option — 'Book a Call' — is tucked at the bottom of the page, after the form, almost as an afterthought. The structure puts the hardest ask first and the easiest ask last. That's backwards.

A visitor doesn't know if Fruuit is the right fit yet. Asking them to declare budget before they've even had a conversation filters out exactly the high-value prospects who evaluate carefully. A 15-minute call is almost always an easier yes than a form that asks for money before trust is built."

---

## BEAT 4 — THE SCORE DROP (20–25 seconds)

*[Pause briefly. Look at camera.]*

"When I put all of this together — the messaging, the structure, the CTAs, all 10 areas I look at — your site scores a 36 out of 100. For context, most ESG consulting sites I've reviewed land somewhere between 30 and 50, so you're in that range.

The good news is that the expertise is clearly there. Your team's background is a real differentiator. It's the translation layer between that expertise and your buyer's decision process that needs work. Firms that fix even two or three of these things usually see a significant jump in qualified conversations coming through their site."

---

## BEAT 5 — THE CLOSE (20 seconds)

*[Look at camera.]*

"I put together a scorecard that breaks down all 10 areas with the scores and the three things I'd prioritize first. I'll drop it in the message along with this video.

If any of this resonates and you want to dig deeper, I'm happy to chat — no pitch, just walk through what the full picture looks like. Either way, hope this was useful. Talk soon."

*[End recording.]*
